App Usage
Parents should be aware of what apps their kids are using and consider the possible risks.

Apps include everything from educational, entertainment, social networking and music.

Understanding Apps

Apps can be free to get while others you pay for once off. Some may include in-app purchases or add-ons. Try and talk to your kid about the apps they’re using, showing interest rather than concern.

One thing to look out for are location settings. You need to consider if it is wise for your kids to post their whereabouts online.

Trolling / Cyberbulling
Lets talk
about online
trolling.
Trolling, also know as cyberbullying, is a form online harassment.

A troll is someone who makes unsolicited or controversial comments with the intent to start
an argument online. The worst trolling happens
on YouTube and social networks such as Instagram, Tumblr and Twitter and is most common in children between the ages of 12 to 16 years.
How to deal with online trolls?

Trolls try to provoke a reaction so you should encourage your child to ignore them. Cyberbullying should be taken more seriously and should be reported to a parent or adult they feel close to. Children should be reminded that if they see something upsetting online they can block or
report that user.

Accessibility / Porn
Let's talk
about porn.
In todays digital world accessing porn is all
too easy as is hiding it.

When porn becomes the primary mode of sexual education it can harm the brain’s sexual development. Porn paints an unrealistic picture of sexuality and relationships that can create an expectation for real-life experiences that will never be fulfilled. Make sure to have good parental controls but you will need to talk to your child about it as they can always find ways around it!
